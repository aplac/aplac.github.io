Bangkok Song Wat Road (s132089)

Bangkok Song Wat Road is a historic road in the Chinatown of Bangkok. It was designed and built in 1892 by King Chulalongkorn, Rama V, after a great fire in the Sampheng area. At a length of more than a kilometer, the road parallels the Chao Praya river and provides an excess to and from the river through storage houses between Song War Road and the river. The Song Wat Road is where important Chinese temples in Bangkok can be found, as well as other early Bangkok buildings and religious sites. E.g. Da Bentou Gong, New Bentou Gong, the Luang Kocha Itsahak Mosque and it\'s associated cemetery.
