姑婆嶼 (s112748)

While underwater quarrying of coral rocks was a common activity on Penghu, the quarrying of basalt stone under water was practices in few areas only, due to the variation of the quality of basalt stones, and very likely restricted through the accessibility of the sea floor in terms of sea currents and the deapth of the water. The advantages of underwater quarry on Penghu were twofold. First, the sea and the sea floor were treated as commons and the stone was thus freely available. No land need to be bought. Second, there is the more general advantage that moving the heavy stone, with a density near 3, was much more easy in water than on land due to the buoyancy. Nevertheless underwater quarrying seems a remarkable and dangereous practice that was maybe practiced by those carving families that could not afford to buy the necessary land. There are traces of working the basalt stone on Gupoyu, as well as on the northern coast line of Baisha.
