s132080: Muslim Cemetery at the Luang Kocha Itsahak Mosque

Name: Muslim Cemetery at the Luang Kocha Itsahak Mosque, Bangkok Muslim Cemetery Song Wat Road, 曼谷穆斯林公墓
Type: cemetery
ID: s132080
Description:
Bangkok Muslim Cemetery at Song Wat Road, behind the Luang Kocha Itsahak Mosque. A pieceful cemetery surrounded by walls and uninhabited houses. As soil marks on the tombstone indicate, this graveyard is suffering from flooding. Some of the tombs, mainly in the north-eastern part of the cemetery are of Chinese Muslims. All of almost all surnamed Ma 馬. Along the backside wall of the cemetery there are tombstones of tombs that have been removed in the past. The conditions why and when tombs have been removed are currently unknown to me. This is certainly a topic of reserach as removing tombs is not something Muslims do easily and without good reason. The graveyard is managed by a family which lives on the east-side of the square in front of the Mosque. Part of the maintenance work includes the daily raking of the leaves. The most common trees on the cemetery are plumeria, also known as frangipani, Chinese: 緬梔花 Mandarin: mian3 zhi1 hua1, Chinese: 蛋黄花 Maindarin dan1 huang2 hua1, Thai: ลั่นทม Lạ̀nthm, which cover the tombs daily with fresh flowers and give a nice fragrance to the entire area. I assume that planting these trees is a tactics of the Muslim community to circumvene the interdiction of bringing flowers to the cemetery. Similar planting tactics have also been observed on Muslim cemeteries in Hong Kong. In the south-eastern corner of the cemetery there are a number of carefully designed and erected tombs. As there are no inscriptions, it is not clear why these tombs are different. Some seem very small, so that one might hypothesize that the tombs are childrens\' tombs. An alternative explanation might be that these are earliest tombs, tombs of the family of Luang Kocha Itsahak, or tombs of Imams.
