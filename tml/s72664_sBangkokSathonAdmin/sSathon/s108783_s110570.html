s110570: Bangkok Chaozhou Cemeterys Fude Ci

Name: Bangkok Chaozhou Cemeterys Fude Ci, 曼谷潮州義山福德祠
Type: Earth God temple, 土地公廟, Taoist temple, 廟, temple, container, three-dimensional object, building, container, three-dimensional object, 建築物, site, 遺址, place, spatial configuration meaningful for humans, prayer site, religious site, site, 遺址, place, spatial configuration meaningful for humans
ID: s110570
Description:
Bangkok Chaozhou Cemetery's Fude Ci, 曼谷潮州義山福德祠, is located at the south-eastern corner of the cemetery. Looking northward, the temple is in one line with the main road that at this points turns from going eastwards to going northwards. The temple has a number of features typical for a Chaozhou temple in Bangkok, e.g. the mast and the altar dedicated to the heaven and the earth. It also includes a tree where decommissioned statues and figurines can be deposed. While the Earth God temple on the Hokkien Cemetery has two crocodile sculls, this Earth God temples features two deer skulls on top of the altar niche. The donar tablet is inscribed in Thai. Documented in 2019 and 2024, it will be interesting to see how this temple has changed, and how our documentation technique has changed, if so.
