挽蒲蟻光炎紀念堂 (s108791)

Bangpoo 蟻光炎 Memorial. 蟻光炎 (1879—1939-11-21) was a leader of overseas Chinese in Thailand and served as the 15th and 16th Chairman of the Chinese General Chamber of Commerce in Thailand. He was a native of of Chenghai (澄海) County, Chaozhou Prefecture, Guangdong Province, China.
Yi was born in 1879 in Nanpanzhou Village, Dongli, Chenghai, Guangdong, China (廣東澄海東里南畔洲村). He grew up in a poor family. When he was 3 years old, his parents and brother died one after another. He was raised by his brother, sister-in-law and sister. In 1895, with the help of his brother and sister-in-law, Yi left his hometown and went to Annan (安南) to make a living.
At the age of 23, Yi arrived in Bangkok, Siam, and co-founded the "Liu Shun Company" (六順公司) with his friends. Later, he independently established Guangxingtai, Guangshunli, Guangshuntai (光興泰, 光順利, 光順泰) and other companies with his own funds. He successively developed industries such as shipping, firewood, sugarcane, rubber, shellac, grain and oil. Later he initiated the establishment of friendship and charity groups such as the Chaozhou Association, the Firewood Association, and the Baodeshan Hall (報德善堂), and served as the chairman of the Chinese General Chamber of Commerce. During his tenure as an overseas Chinese leader, Yi organized many public welfare projects such as fundraising, disaster relief, education, and medical services serving the overseas Chinese community.
In 1937, during the Chinese War of Resistance against Japanese Aggression, Yi called for a boycott of Japanese goods and led the promotion of national salvation bonds and fundraising activities. In 1938, Yi mobilized overseas Chinese mechanics to transport anti-Japanese supplies along the Yunnan-Myanmar Highway and founded the Zhongyuan Daily (中原報) in Bangkok to spread anti-Japanese propaganda. On the evening of November 21, 1939, Yi was assassinated on Yaowarat Road in Bangkok at the age of 61.
