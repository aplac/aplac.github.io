Xiyu Island (s113840)

Xiyu Island is an island of the Penghu Archipelago. It\'s name means Western Island a name obviously derived from its position to the west of the Penghu Island and Magong, the captial. Xiyu had an probably still has a miliatry importants as from the high cliffs on the South of Xiyu, the entrance to the entrance to the inner circle of the Main Penghu Islands, Penghu, Baisha and Xiyu, and thus the acccess to Magong can be controlled. Numerous military structures have been installed or maintained and used on Xiyu by the Qing, by the Japanese Empirial Army and the Republic of China under the Kuomintang. The presence of soldiers during the Qing on Penghu reflects maybe partially through the relative absence of a Jiguan on the tombstones and the relative prevalence of a loyalty expression to the Qing, 皇清. It is on Xiyu subsequently that the first tombstones with a 堂號 appear on Penghu after the Japanese occupation of Penghu in 1895. From Xiyu the practice of carving a Tanghao spread over to other islands of Xiyu.
The practice of the five generals on Xiyu is also slightly different than on other main islands of Penghu. While on Baisha and in Magong, the five generals have been generally renovated and converted into small temples, the five generals on Xiyu are still in a relative simple form of a few bamboo sticks, representing the generals, fixed in a small concrete structures. Different are also the two villages of Holuo and Hengjiao, where the five generals are turned by 90 degrees. This is relatively uncommon and matched only by the village Hongluo on Xiyu Island, where the generals are turned by 180 degrees, meaning that the north general is in the south of the village, the west general in the east etc. The cause for these unexpected locations of the general are still unknown to me. It is known that the location of the generals is determined by the spirit medium, Tongji in Taiwanese, or Jitong in Mandarin. What made the Jitong turn the directions is difficult to know unless more cases of such unexpected locations can be found, on Penghu or elsewhere. It is very unlikely that this unexpected location of the generals happened by mistake, as Penghu people, through the winter monsoon are reminded very clearly where is north and where is south.
