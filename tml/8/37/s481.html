馬公孤拔和部下的墓的紀念碑 (s481)

The first site we visited on Penghu. It indicates the place where the tomb of Admiral Courbet is supposed to have been. A copy of the tomb is now located at Shetou Shan (Snakehead Hill) near Fenggui, Magong. While the body of Admiral Courbet had been relocated in 1885 to France, the bodies of his officers were relocated in 1954 to the French Graveyard in Keelung. It can be assumed that the photos of the graveyard taken during the Japanese period show the original gravemarkers. The biggest marker, presumably that of Admiral Courbet is in a style similar to that we find with graveyardmakers of the 19th century in the Hong Kong Cemetery for mainly British citizens. Who designed and built the gravemarkers in this site is not known to us. The copy found at Shetou Shan and which might integrate some original slabs, is made of granite, a stone not found on Penghu. It thus might be the case that the entire marker has been shipped to Penghu. A comparison with French colonial tombs in Vietnam might be of interest to shed some light on this marker.
