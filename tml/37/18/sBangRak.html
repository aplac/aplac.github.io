s134385: Silom Parsi Cemetery

Name:
Silom Parsi Cemetery, Bang Rak Parsi Cemetery
Type: cemetery, Parsi cemetery
ID: s134385
Description:
The Silom Parsi Cemetery is a Parsi cemetery in direct vicinity to the Hakka, Hokkien and Cantonese Cemeteries. To enter the cemetery one has to enter the alley to the Cantonese school and push a rolling gate open, which is unlocked during day time. A gardien is living insight the area of the Christian and Parsi Cemetery and he will lock and unlock the cemetery. Within the compound, the cemetery is separated by a fence in two parts, the Christian Cemetery and the Parsi Cemetery. Within the Parsi cemeteries there are a few older Parsi tombs, while newer tombs with Christian or Muslim symbolisms are included. In the center of the areal of the Parsi cemetery is a monument commenmorating the establishment of the cemetery. A simple wooden house on the compound of the Parsi cemetery is where the gardien of the cemeteris lives during day time.

sBangkokBangRakGuangdongCemetery: Bangkok Guangdong Cemetery

Name:
Bangkok Guangdong Cemetery, 曼谷廣東義山
Type: cemetery
ID: sBangkokBangRakGuangdongCemetery
Description:
The Bangkok Guangdong Cemetery (曼谷廣東墳場) is located at Silom Road, at the backside of the Cantonese school, close to the Hakka and the Hokkien graveyard. The cemetery is used as a commercial parking lot. On the premises of the graveyard there is also an Earthgod temple, which cannot be entered though. Not only people from Guangtong are buried here. It might be the case that the tombs not from Guangdong date from a time when the cemetery was not yet specifically dedicated to the Guangdong community. Likewise one can find Qing-Dynasty Hokkien tombs on the Hakka graveyard. A more detailed study of this site and the other sites is thus required, looking especially at the earliest tombs.
In 2023 or before, a 5m high metal fence has been set up to prevent people from entering the cemetery and from viewing what is going on in the cemetery. People entereing the cemetery are threatened with violence. Cars are still parked in the first part of the cemetery. They seem to belong to a special clientel as they can enter and leave freely. Cameras have been installed to control who enters the cemetery.
