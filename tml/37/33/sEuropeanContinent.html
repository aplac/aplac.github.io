sBelgium: Belgien

Name:
Belgien, Belgium, Belgique, 比利時
Type: administrative region
ID: sBelgium
Description:
Belgium, situated at the crossroads of Western Europe, presents a fascinating linguistic mosaic. The northern region (Flanders) is predominantly Dutch-speaking, while the southern region (Wallonia) is primarily French-speaking, with a small German-speaking community nestled along the eastern border with Germany. Brussels, the capital, presents an intriguing linguistic anomaly: despite being geographically surrounded by Dutch-speaking territories, it functions largely as a French-speaking enclave—a linguistic island shaped by the historical prestige French maintained throughout the region for centuries.
Urban centers like Brussels have become important sites for linguistic landscape research, where scholars analyze public written language on signs, advertisements, and official notices to understand language distribution and power dynamics. However, these conventional linguistic landscape methodologies primarily capture formal, public language usage while often overlooking private linguistic practices.
.The Thakbong project expands traditional linguistic landscape research by examining language choices in burial sites and prayer spaces—intimate domains where cultural identity, personal history, and communal belonging intersect. By documenting linguistic practices in these sacred spaces, the project reveals dimensions of language use that remain invisible in standard public signage studies, offering deeper insights into authentic community language practices and heritage preservation.
