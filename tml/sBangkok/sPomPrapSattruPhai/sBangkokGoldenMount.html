Description:
The Golden Mount is a temple structure, a burial site and a stupa built on the ruins of a former temple in the Pom Prap Sattru Phai District of Bangkok, Thailand. It is administered by the Wat Saket which lies to the East and South of the Mount. In the 19th century, the mount was a site for sky burials when epidemics struck the city and deceased couldn't be burried or cremated due to the large number of people that fell victim to the epidemics.
Most tombs that can be found today on the Golden Mount are tombs of ethnic Chinese. These tombs date mainly from the first half of the 20th century. It may be assumed that the mount with canals running on two sides of the mount created a favorable fengshui that made this site favorable for ethnic Chinese. In addition, most shops on the Boriphat Road are of ethnic Chinese, most of them involved in processing and trading wood, with a historic focus on teak wood that has been shipped from the north of Thailand.
