Chiang MaiProvince is located in the north-west of Thailand, bordering to its north and east with Myanmar. Chiang Mai is home to numerous Indigenous groups, Thai people, ethnic Chinese, mainly Chaozhou people, who arrived from the south of Thailand, Muslim Chinese who arrived via Myanmar from Yunnan after the Panthay Rebellion (1856–1873), also known as the Du Wenxiu Rebellion (Tu Wen-hsiu Rebellion) in the south-west of Yunnan, as well as Kuomintang Soldiers and their offspring, who live in relative autonomy in about 100 settlements in the mountainous border region.
