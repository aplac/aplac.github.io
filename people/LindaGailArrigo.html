Prof. Linda Gail Arrigo

台北醫學大學退休教授
Taipei Medical University (retired)
Thakbong Project

In 2007, Prof Linda Gail Arrigo started to take out her students of the Taipei Medical University to a nearby complex of gravesites, including the burial grounds of the White Terror of Kuomintang in Taiwan, a Muslim graveyard of Mainlanders who arrived on Taiwan in the aftermath of the Chinese Civil War and the Fude-Graveyard, a graveyard of mainly Mainlanders, but also local Taiwanese.

In 2010, Oliver Streiter and Yoann Goudin contacted Linda Gail Arrigo and they arranged a first meeting in the autumn of 2010. After Yoann returned to France, Oliver and Linda continued to visit and document various gravesites in the Taipei area between 2010 and 2018. Frequently a fieldtrip was staged after Linda, who likes to hike the mountainous surrounding of Taipei, had discovered a hence unknown burial ground or some tombs spread along the trail.

In 2011 Linda Gail Arrigo and Oliver Streiter organized the First Conference on Documenting and Researching Gravesites in Taiwan. Already in 2011, the impending disappearance of burial sites in Taiwan and the cultural and historical loss this would imply for a new nation.
